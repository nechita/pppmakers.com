You are probably wondering, why whould you give out discounts to people based on their location?

It's a legitimate question, so I got a few possible reasons for which you might make this an important part of your pricing strategy. But before that, let me make you understand what it's like to live in a country with a poor global purchasing power.

I love to learn new stuff, and in my search for the best learning material I end up on a lot of premium courses landing pages.

Most of these courses have fair prices, but usually the pricing is tailored for people in more affluent countries like North America, Western & Northen Europe and a few other countries scattered accross the globe.

My country is anything but affluent, so the financial strain to educate myself by using the best resources on the web is quit big.

Thanks to the makers & businesses you will find on this website, me and other curious minds from around the world benefit from location-based discounts, that make it more affordable for us to learn and create stuff.

For me, these discounts are the difference between buying something or not, so for you a 50% discount off the full price for someone located in Easter Europe, might mean the difference between making $0 or 50% on a sale. If you sell something that has low hosting costs or is easy to "duplicate the product", your bottom line will not suffer, it might actually grow as a consequence of creating a more inclusive pricing strategy.

A few good reasons why you would want to integrate PPP in your pricing strategy:

1. You will get rid off sob stories from people asking for a discount.

2. You will earn new clients that you might scare away with your pricing.

3. You signal to your audience that you understand and you care for the fact that not all people can afford your courses.

4. Free advertising from websites like this one :).

5. Free advertising from excited new customers tweeting about the fact that you have adapted pricing based on PPP :).
